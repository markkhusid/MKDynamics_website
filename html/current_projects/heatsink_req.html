Raspberry Pi 3 Supercomputer Heatsink Requirements

In what follows we will calculate the required thermal impedance of the heatsinking mechanism for the Raspberry Pi 3 Supercomputer. From previous calculations, it was observed that the Junction to Ambient Thermal Impedance of the CPU was about 32 C/W. Our goal is maintain the CPU temperature below 80C in order to prevent throttling of the CPU's clock rate by the built-in thermal governor.

In what follows, we will calculate the required thermal impedance of the heatsink. Under heavy load, the Raspberry Pi 3 is consuming 3.9W.  We will assume that all of this is consumed by the CPU. Under these conditions, the temperature rise of the junction is:

Temperature rise of junction due to power dissipation across Junction to Ambient Thermal Impedance [deg C]

(%i35) T_rise_heavy_load_RthJA: P_RPi3_4_core_heavy_load * Theta_JA_RPI3_CPU;
\[\mathrm{\tt (\%o35) }\quad 125.2415094339623\]

The portion of junction temperature rise due to the junction to case thermal impedance is:

Temperature rise of junction due to power dissipation across the Junction to Case Thermal Impedance [deg C]

(%i36) T_rise_heavy_load_RthJC: P_RPi3_4_core_heavy_load * Theta_JC_RPi3_CPU;
\[\mathrm{\tt (\%o36) }\quad 66.37358490566038\]

Define the maximum junction temperature that we will allow to prevent thermal throttling of CPU clock rate. We will arbitrarily set the maximum temperature to 75C, to allow a 10C margin under 85C for manufacturing tolerances.

Maximum allowed junction temperature [deg C]

(%i37) T_junction_allowed_max: 75;
\[\mathrm{\tt (\%o37) }\quad 75\]

The maximum allowed temperature rise is: [deg C]

(%i38) T_rise_allowed_max: T_junction_allowed_max - T_rise_heavy_load_RthJC;
\[\mathrm{\tt (\%o38) }\quad 8.62641509433962\]

This temperature rise has to occur while dissipating 3.9W, which implies that the thermal impedance of the heatsink must be:

Thermal Impedance of Heatsink (Case to Ambient) [C/W]

(%i39) R_theta_heatsink_ambient: T_rise_allowed_max / P_RPi3_4_core_heavy_load;
\[\mathrm{\tt (\%o39) }\quad 2.211901306240928\]
